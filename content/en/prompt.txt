However, unasanu doesn't require the many-worlds interpretation of quantum mechanics, the infinity of our universe, or the Omega Point hypothesis. This article describes the same idea as Max Tegmark's Mathematical Universe Hypothesis [\[1\]] or Greg Egan's dust theory [\[2\]], but with a greater number of implications and ideas that these authors did not consider or only touched upon superficially.

Core ideas:
```
* Universes inside simulations exist for themselves.
    * Because they are deterministic.
    * Because the result of their computation can be represented by a number, and all numbers exist.
    * Therefore, it is not necessary to simulate them.
    * And if their simulation is stopped, they will continue to exist.
    * By simulating, we do not create a world, but observe an already existing one.
* Computations exist thanks to observers.
* For simulations, all moments of time exist, and there is no objective flow of time.
* There exist simulations not only with a naive model of time, but also with any other.
* There exist not all imaginable worlds, but all constructible ones (construction principle).
* In simulations that exist for themselves, an infinite number of computations and memory are available.
* There exist the following unusual classes of universes:
    * With all possible interventions.
    * Infinite universes with a limit on the maximum speed of information propagation.
    * Some types of continuum universes that can be simulated by the limit transition simulation method or similar methods.
    * Universes with absolute randomness.
    * Universes that can solve the halting problem.
* Consciousness is also a separate "universe" and exists as a number, for itself.
* In a stone, consciousness is encoded, but this makes little sense.
* Any algorithm has qualia.
* We simultaneously exist in an infinite number of simulations and are simultaneously an independent reality.
* Any universe has an infinite number of gods.
* No god can prohibit some universe from existing.
* The god who created all universes makes no sense.
* Any experience must be experienced (critique of the measure problem).
* It is impossible to die.
* It is possible to change the universe by one's own will.
    * Probably, aliens do just that.
* Unasanu is unfalsifiable, but this is not a death sentence.
* Occam's Razor can be applied in two contradictory ways.
```
